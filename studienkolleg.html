Who should attend Studienkolleg?

Studienkolleg is designed for international students whose school-leaving certificates are not directly recognized for university admission in Germany. This typically includes students from education systems that have less than 13 years of schooling or whose curriculum doesn't match German standards.

You should consider Studienkolleg if:

  • Your secondary school diploma isn't equivalent to the German Abitur
  • You need to improve your German language skills for academic studies
  • You want to bridge subject-specific knowledge gaps

Studienkolleg Types

There are different types of Studienkollegs in Germany, categorized by the university degree you plan to pursue:

  • M-Kurs: For medical, biological, and pharmaceutical studies
  • T-Kurs: For mathematical, scientific, or technical studies
  • W-Kurs: For business, economics, and social science studies
  • G-Kurs: For humanities studies or German studies
  • S-Kurs: For language studies (less common)

Courses Offered

Studienkolleg courses typically last two semesters (one year) and include:

  • German language (6-8 hours per week)
  • Subject-specific courses depending on your chosen course type
  • Mathematics and natural sciences for technical courses
  • Social sciences and economics for business courses

At the end of the program, students take the Feststellungsprüfung (university qualification exam).

Costs and Fees

Studienkolleg costs vary depending on the institution:

  • Public Studienkollegs: Typically charge €200-€500 per semester (similar to university fees)
  • Private Studienkollegs: Can cost €3,000-€6,000 per semester
  • Additional costs: Health insurance (~€120/month), living expenses (~€850/month), books and materials

Some states in Germany charge no tuition fees for Studienkolleg.

Application Process

The application process typically involves:

  1. Choosing the appropriate Studienkolleg course type
  2. Meeting the German language requirement (usually B1/B2 level)
  3. Submitting your secondary school certificates
  4. Passing the entrance examination (Aufnahmeprüfung)
  5. Applying for a student visa if required

Application deadlines are usually 3-6 months before the semester starts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I skip Studienkolleg?

You may skip Studienkolleg if your secondary education is recognized as equivalent to the German Abitur or if you have completed 1-2 years of university studies in your home country.

What's the pass rate for the final exam?

The Feststellungsprüfung pass rate is typically 70-80% across public Studienkollegs.

Can I work while attending Studienkolleg?

Yes, with a student visa you're allowed to work 120 full days or 240 half days per year.
