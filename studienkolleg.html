Who should attend Studienkolleg?

Studienkolleg is designed for international students whose school-leaving certificates are not directly recognized for university admission in Germany. This typically includes students from education systems that have less than 13 years of schooling or whose curriculum doesn't match German standards.

You should consider Studienkolleg if:

  • Your secondary school diploma isn't equivalent to the German Abitur
  • You need to improve your German language skills for academic studies
  • You want to bridge subject-specific knowledge gaps

Studienkolleg Types

There are different types of Studienkollegs in Germany, categorized by the university degree you plan to pursue:

  • M-Kurs: For medical, biological, and pharmaceutical studies
  • T-Kurs: For mathematical, scientific, or technical studies
  • W-Kurs: For business, economics, and social science studies
  • G-Kurs: For humanities studies or German studies
  • S-Kurs: For language studies (less common)

Courses Offered

Studienkolleg courses typically last two semesters (one year) and include:

  • German language (6-8 hours per week)
  • Subject-specific courses depending on your chosen course type
  • Mathematics and natural sciences for technical courses
  • Social sciences and economics for business courses
